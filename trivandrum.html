Thiruvananthapuram

Clockwise, from top: View of Kulathoor, Padmanabhaswamy Temple, Niyamasabha Mandiram, East Fort, Technopark, Kanakakkunnu Palace, Thiruvananthapuram Central and Kovalam Beach

Thiruvananthapuram (/ˌtɪruːvəˌnʌntəˈpʊərəm/), formerly known as Trivandrum (/trɪˈvændrəm/), is the capital of the state of Kerala.
It is the most populous city in Kerala with a population of 957,730 as of 2011.
Located on the west coast of India near the extreme south of the mainland, Thiruvananthapuram is a major information technology hub in Kerala and contributes 55% of the state's software exports as of 2016.
Referred to by Mahatma Gandhi as the "Evergreen city of India", the city is characterised by its undulating terrain of low coastal hills.
Thiruvananthapuram is a notable academic and research hub and home to the University of Kerala, APJ Abdul Kalam Technological University, the regional headquarters of Indira Gandhi National Open University, and many other schools and colleges.
Thiruvananthapuram is also home to research centers such as the National Institute for Interdisciplinary Science and Technology, Indian Space Research Organisation's Vikram Sarabhai Space Centre, the Indian Institute of Space Science and Technology, National Centre for Earth Science Studies and a campus of the Indian Institutes of Science Education and Research.
The city is home to media institutions like Toonz India Ltd and Tata Elxsi Ltd, and is also home to Chitranjali Film Studio, one of the first film studios in Malayalam Cinema, and Kinfra Film and Video Park at Kazhakoottom, which is India's first Infotainment industrial park.
Being India's largest city in the deep south, it is strategically prominent and hosts the Southern Air Command headquarters of the Indian Air Force, the Thumba Equatorial Rocket Launching Station and the Vizhinjam International Seaport.
Thiruvananthapuram is a major tourist centre, known for the Padmanabhaswamy Temple, the beaches of Kovalam and Varkala, the backwaters of Poovar and Anchuthengu and its Western Ghats tracts of Ponmudi and the Agastya Mala.
In 2012, Thiruvananthapuram was named the best Kerala city to live in, by a field survey conducted by The Times of India.

Some of the main attractions of Trivandrum are
  • Shree Padmanabhaswamy Temple

    • The Padmanabhaswamy Temple,richest temple of the world, treasure vaults holds fortunes enough to save the world from its various issues but the treasures are believed bound by an ancient curse.
    There are many other temples famous in trivandrum; such as Attukal Bhagavathy Temple which holds Guinness Book of World Records when 1.5 million women participated in Pongala.

  • Beach

    • There are a lot of beaches in Trivandrum. Some of the main beaches of tourist attraction are Varkala, Shanghumukham, Kovalam.

  • Museum and Zoo

    • Trivandrum consists of a wide variety of museums. some of the must see and feel are Napier, Sunil's Wax, Word, Airforce, H.H.Uthradam thirunal, Aircraft, Sri Chitra, History etc.
    The zoo consists of wide variety of flora and fauna which is located in the heart of the city.

  • Magic Planet

    • Magic Planet- the museum of magic, is totally a new concept located at the Kinfra Film and Video Park situated at Kazhakuttom, Thiruvananthapuram, Kerala. A multitude of fascinating activities awaits visitors here. The magic castle spreads over 1.5 acres encircled by natural beauty which holds a number of attractions.

  • Ponmudi

    • Ponmudi also known as Ooty of South Kerala, Kashmir of Kerala is a hill station in the Peringamala gramapanchayath of Trivandrum. Ponmudi situated 61 km from Thiruvananthapuram city is an enchanting hill station destination famous for its mist covered hills.
